description: Steelman and challenge a note or idea to stress-test it
---

Take a note or idea and challenge it rigorously — find the weakest assumptions, strongest objections, and missing evidence.

## Steps

1. Read the note or idea I specify (or ask me to paste it).
2. **Steelman first**: articulate the strongest possible version of the argument — better than I stated it.
3. **Challenge from three angles:**

   **A. Assumption attack**
   - What is this idea taking for granted?
   - Which assumption, if false, would collapse the whole argument?

   **B. Counter-evidence**
   - What evidence or examples contradict this idea?
   - Where does this idea break down or fail?

   **C. Scope creep**
   - Is this idea being applied beyond where it's valid?
   - What contexts does it NOT apply to?

4. **Rate the idea** (1–5) on:
   - Originality: how novel is this?
   - Robustness: how well does it survive challenge?
   - Usefulness: how actionable or generative is it?

5. **Recommended action:**
   - Strengthen by addressing: [specific weakness]
   - OR: graduate as-is because it survived well
   - OR: archive — superseded by [stronger idea]

Be direct. Flattery does not strengthen ideas.
